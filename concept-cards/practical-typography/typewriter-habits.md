# Quick Definition

Typewriter habits are obsolete typographic conventions inherited from the mechanical constraints of typewriters — including straight quotes, double sentence spacing, 12pt defaults, and many others — that persist unnecessarily in digital documents.

# Core Definition

Butterick catalogs 22 "bad typography habits left over from the typewriter era," arising "from necessity, not because anyone liked them." Typewriters had limited character sets and mechanical constraints (e.g., fixed-width characters, limited vertical movement) that forced compromises. These compromises became entrenched habits that persist in digital documents despite no longer being necessary.

# Prerequisites

- **Typography** — Understanding what typography is before understanding what degrades it.

# Key Properties

1. **22 specific habits**: Butterick catalogs them comprehensively.
2. **Arose from constraints**: "From necessity, not because anyone liked them."
3. **Mechanical origins**: Limited character sets, fixed-width type, single-increment line platen.
4. **Persist unnecessarily**: Word processors have had superior capabilities for decades.
5. **Key offenders**: Straight quotes, double sentence spacing, hyphen-for-dash substitution, 12pt default, single/double line spacing.

# Construction / Recognition

## To Identify/Recognise:
1. Straight quotes instead of curly quotes.
2. Two spaces between sentences.
3. Hyphens used instead of en/em dashes.
4. All text at 12pt.
5. Only single or double line spacing.
6. Monospaced fonts for body text.
7. Underlining for emphasis instead of italics or bold.
8. Excessive use of all caps.
9. Alphabetic approximations of trademark/copyright symbols.

# Context & Application

- **Typical contexts**: Documents created by anyone who learned to type on a typewriter or was taught by someone who did.
- **Common applications**: Quality audit checklist — systematically eliminating typewriter habits is a quick path to better typography.
- **Historical note**: Robin Williams's *The Mac is Not a Typewriter* (early Mac era) was one of the first popular books to address this.

# Examples

**Example 1** (typewriter-habits): "Were typewriters ever used to typeset books, magazines, or newspapers? Nope." — The conventions that arose from typewriter limitations were never used in professional typography.

**Example 2** (typewriter-habits): The 12pt default — "Thinking that the best point size for body text is 12" is listed as typewriter habit #17.

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Treating typewriter conventions as typographic rules.
  **Correction**: Typewriter conventions were mechanical compromises. Professional typography has always used curly quotes, proper dashes, varied line spacing, etc.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: "I was taught to do it this way, so it must be correct."
  **Clarification**: Being taught a typewriter convention doesn't make it a typographic rule. "Were typewriters ever used to typeset books, magazines, or newspapers? Nope."
