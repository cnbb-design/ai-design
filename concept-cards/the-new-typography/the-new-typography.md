# Quick Definition

The New Typography is a modernist design movement (c. 1925-1930) that replaced decorative, centred typography with functional, asymmetric design whose form grows out of the content's communicative purpose — "The essence of the New Typography is clarity."

# Core Definition

Tschichold's manifesto defines the movement through its core thesis: "The essence of the New Typography is clarity. This puts it into deliberate opposition to the old typography whose aim was 'beauty' and whose clarity did not attain the high level we require today."

The movement originated from the collective work of avant-garde artists across Europe — Baumeister, Bayer, Lissitzky, Moholy-Nagy, Schwitters, Zwart — who applied the formal principles of abstract painting and constructivism to typography. As Tschichold states: "The break with the old typography, made complete by the new movement, means nothing less than the total discarding of decorative concepts and the turn to functional design."

The New Typography addresses the conditions of modern communication: "Modern man has to absorb every day a mass of printed matter... The speed with which the modern consumer of printing has to absorb it means that the form of printing also must adapt itself to the conditions of modern life."

# Key Properties

1. **Clarity as essence**: The primary goal is clear communication, not beauty or decoration.
2. **Functional form**: "The New Typography is distinguished from the old by the fact that its first objective is to develop its visible form out of the functions of the text."
3. **Asymmetric design**: Replaces centred/axial typography with asymmetric composition.
4. **Contemporary expression**: "Every piece of typography which originates in a preconceived idea of form, of whatever kind, is wrong."
5. **Collective, not individual**: The movement emerged from collective effort, not individual genius.
6. **Not a fashion**: "It would be naive and short-sighted to think that the New Typography... is only a temporary fashion."

# Construction / Recognition

## To Construct/Create:
1. Start from the content's communicative purpose, not from a preconceived visual form.
2. Use asymmetric layout driven by reading order and emphasis.
3. Eliminate decoration and ornament.
4. Deploy contrast (size, weight, colour) to express the logical structure of the text.
5. Treat white space as an active design element.

## To Identify/Recognise:
1. Asymmetric, left-aligned or dynamically composed layouts.
2. Sans-serif typefaces, strong size contrasts, functional use of rules and colour.
3. Integration of photography with typography.
4. Absence of decorative ornament.

# Context & Application

- **Typical contexts**: All forms of printed communication — letterheads, posters, books, advertisements, periodicals.
- **Common applications**: The principles remain foundational to modern graphic design and web design.
- **Historical note**: Published June 1928 in Munich. Tschichold later moderated his position, acknowledging the value of traditional typography for certain contexts (notably book design), but the movement's core principles — clarity, function, asymmetry — became the foundation of 20th-century graphic design.

# Examples

**Example 1** (p. 64): Tschichold's introduction states the book's purpose: "to say something more about this new way of viewing our world, in which our spiritual conception of the new forms are linked with the whole range of human activity."

**Example 2** (p. 66): The movement is tied to its era's broader modernism: "the birth of a new and splendid architecture, which will set its stamp on our period. Anyone who has recognized the deep underlying similarity between typography and architecture... can no longer doubt that the future will belong to the new typography."

# Common Errors

- **Error**: Adopting external features (bold rules, circles, triangles) as new decoration.
  **Correction**: "If these are merely substituted for the old ornaments, nothing is improved." Geometric forms must be functional, not decorative.

# Common Confusions

- **Confusion**: The New Typography is a style or fashion to be copied.
  **Clarification**: "Merely to copy its external shapes would be to create a new formalism as bad as the old." It is a method of working from function to form, not a set of visual rules.
