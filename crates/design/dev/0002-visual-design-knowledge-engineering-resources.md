# Visual Design Knowledge Engineering — Resources

Best-of-breed sources for concept card extraction, organized by domain.

---

## 1. Colour Theory & Colour Systems

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1963 | Josef Albers | *Interaction of Color* | Canonical text demonstrating colour as a relative medium — behaviour depends entirely on context. |
| 1961 | Johannes Itten | *The Art of Color* | Bauhaus-rooted systematic framework: seven colour contrasts, harmonies, and subjective experience. |
| 1905 | Albert Munsell | *Munsell Color System* | Scientific colour model with hue, value, and chroma as independent axes in a 3D coordinate system. |
| 2021 | Björn Ottosson | *OKLCH / OKLAB Colour Spaces* | Perceptually uniform colour spaces that fix broken assumptions in HSL; essential for code-driven colour. |
| ? | Sarah Drasner | "A Nerd's Guide to Color on the Web" | Bridges colour theory and CSS/web implementation for practitioners. |

## 2. Typography & Typographic Systems

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1992 | Robert Bringhurst | *The Elements of Typographic Style* | Definitive typographic reference covering rhythm, proportion, form, and page architecture. |
| 2004 | Ellen Lupton | *Thinking with Type* | Accessible, modern, and web-aware applied typography guide. |
| ? | Tim Brown | *Modular Scale* | Mathematical approach to type sizing based on musical intervals and ratios. |
| 2010 | Matthew Butterick | *Practical Typography* | Opinionated, concise, web-native typography reference (practicaltypography.com). |
| ? | Google | *Google Fonts Knowledge* | Surprisingly deep resource on variable fonts, optical sizing, and type selection (fonts.google.com/knowledge). |

## 3. Layout, Composition & Spatial Design

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1928 | Jan Tschichold | *The New Typography* | Historical foundation for asymmetric layout and hierarchy through position and scale. |
| 1981 | Josef Müller-Brockmann | *Grid Systems in Graphic Design* | The Swiss grid bible — mathematical grid construction, multi-column and modular grids. |
| 2010 | Massimo Vignelli | *The Vignelli Canon* | Concise modernist design philosophy covering semantic, syntactic, and pragmatic considerations (free PDF). |
| ? | Heydon Pickering & Andy Bell | *Every Layout* | Modern CSS layout patterns derived from intrinsic design principles, not arbitrary breakpoints (every-layout.dev). |

## 4. UI/UX Principles & Interaction Design

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1988 | Don Norman | *The Design of Everyday Things* | Foundational cognitive science layer: mental models, affordances, signifiers, mapping, and feedback. |
| 2000 | Steve Krug | *Don't Make Me Think* | Core heuristics of web usability, elegantly distilled. |
| ? | Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger | *Refactoring UI* | Tactical, visual, pattern-oriented design guidance written explicitly for developers (refactoringui.com). |
| ? | Nielsen Norman Group | *nngroup.com* | Decades of evidence-based UX research; articles are essentially pre-made concept cards. |
| 2019 | Jon Yablonski | *Laws of UX* | Psychological principles underlying interface design — Fitts's, Hick's, Jakob's, Miller's Laws, etc. (lawsofux.com). |

## 5. Design Systems & Component Architecture

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 2016 | Brad Frost | *Atomic Design* | Systematic decomposition of interfaces: atoms → molecules → organisms → templates → pages. |
| ? | Nathan Curtis | *Writing on Design Systems* | Engineering-rigorous approach to design tokens, spacing scales, and system governance (Medium). |
| ? | Google / IBM / Shopify / GitHub | *Material Design 3 / Carbon / Polaris / Primer* | Open-source design systems as living, well-documented implementations of design theory. |

## 6. Visual & Gestalt Psychology

| Year | Author(s) | Work Title | Description |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1954 | Rudolf Arnheim | *Art and Visual Perception* | How vision works in the context of art and design, grounding Gestalt principles in perceptual psychology. |
| 2004 | Colin Ware | *Information Visualization: Perception for Design* | Scientific foundation for why certain visual encodings work; data-viz focused but universally applicable. |
